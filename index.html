How does it work?

The protocol is based on a series of complex interactions, with the behavior of multiple

stakeholders deciding the pricing dynamics of the multiple tokens of the ecosystem.

When ATomb price is above the peg, new ATomb is minted through the protocol to inflate the

supply, in an attempt to drive the price down towards the peg. The new ATomb tokens are

placed into circulation through the Crypt and distributed to ASHARE holders. This increases

the demand for ASHARE, thus increasing the price and value of ASHARE tokens.

When ATomb price drops below the peg, the protocol will allow minting ABonds with ATomb

(up to a maximum debt limit). This removes Atomb from the total supply, applying upward

pressure on the price towards the peg. ABonds can then be redeemed for ATomb at a

premium when the price is above peg in the future.

What are ABOND (Bonds)?

Bonds are unique tokens that can be utilized to help stabilize

ATomb price around peg (2,000 ATomb = 1 ETH) by reducing

circulating supply of ATomb if the TWAP (time-weighted-average-

price) goes below peg (2,000 ATomb = 1 ETH).

When can I buy

ABOND (Bonds)?

ABONDs can be purchased only on contraction periods, when TWAP

of ATomb is below 1.0. Every new epoch on contraction periods,

ABONDs are issued in the amount of 3% of current ATOMB

circulating supply, with a max debt amount of 45%.

This means that if bonds reach 45% of circulating supply of ATomb,

no more bonds will be issued.
